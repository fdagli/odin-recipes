Kebabs

Kebab is a type of cooked meat dish that originates from cuisines of the Middle East.

Many variants of the category are popular around the world, including the skewered shish kebab and the doner kebab with bread.

Below we have compiled some kebab recipes that are popular in Anatolia for you.

Adana Kebap

Adana kebap (or kiyma kebabi) is a long, hand-minced meat kebab mounted on a wide iron skewer and grilled over charcoal. Named after the Turkish city of Adana, the kebab is generally "hot" or piquant. The traditional Adana kebab is made using lamb, with a high fatty content cooked over hot coals. Only three ingredients are used in a proper Adana kebab, minced lamb, red capsicum (pepper) and salt.
